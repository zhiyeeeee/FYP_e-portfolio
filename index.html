My Final Year Project

Project Title

A Preference-Based Optimization Model for University Badminton Court Scheduling Using a Genetic Algorithm


Project Background

Badminton is one of the most popular sports among university students. However, increasing demand for badminton courts often leads to scheduling conflicts, duplicate bookings and unfair allocations. Moreover, previous studies didn't consider the user preferences in court scheduling. Therefore, this study develops a preference-based optimization model using a Genetic Algorithm to maximize user satisfaction while minimizing scheduling conflicts.


Research Gap

Most previous studies focused on reducing scheduling conflicts and improving court utilization but did not consider user preferences in badminton court scheduling. Therefore, there is limited research that integrates Genetic Algorithm with user preferences for university badminton court scheduling.


General Objective (RO1)

To maximize user preference satisfaction while ensuring efficient and fair badminton court scheduling by developing and evaluating a preference-based optimization model.


Specific Objectives

  • RO2: To identify the objective function and constraints in badminton court scheduling that satisfy user preferences.
  • RO3: To solve the preference-based badminton court scheduling model using Genetic Algorithm.
  • RO4: To evaluate the performance of the proposed model based on user preference satisfaction, penalty (conflicts), fitness value and court utilization.

Research Significance

  • Increases user satisfaction through preference-based scheduling.
  • Improves operational efficiency in badminton court scheduling.
  • Reduces booking conflicts and duplicate allocations.
  • Demonstrates a real-world application of Genetic Algorithm in sports scheduling.

Research Methodology

  1. Identify problem and conduct literature review on sports scheduling and Genetic Algorithm.
  2. Develop mathematical formulation, objective function and scheduling constraints.
  3. Design and implement Genetic Algorithm using Python.
  4. Conduct experiments using five datasets with different numbers of users (30, 50, 100, 300 and 500 users).
  5. Evaluate model performance based on user preference satisfaction, conflicts, fitness value and court utilization.

Figure 1: Genetic Algorithm Flowchart

Figure 1 shows the workflow of the proposed Genetic Algorithm for solving the badminton court scheduling problem. The proposed Genetic Algorithm begins with random court allocation generation, followed by fitness evaluation, tournament selection, single-point crossover and random mutation until the maximum generation is reached.

Figure 4: Comparison of GA and SA Fitness Values

Figure 4 compares the fitness values obtained by the Genetic Algorithm and Simulated Annealing for different numbers of users.

  • Conflict-free schedules were generated for datasets of 30, 50 and 100 users.
  • Court utilization increased as the number of users increased.
  • The Genetic Algorithm consistently converged to high-quality solutions within 150 and 200 generations.
  • Simulated Annealing outperformed the Genetic Algorithm for larger datasets.
  • Higher booking demand resulted in scheduling conflicts and negative fitness values.

Challenges and Limitations

  • Five fixed datasets were used.
  • User preference data were randomly generated and may not fully represent actual university booking behaviour.
  • The study only considered six badminton courts.
  • The model used a single-objective optimization approach.
